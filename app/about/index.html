What is ITLS?

Bridging the Gap between Research and Practice

At ITLS, we’re excited about innovation. How do people learn in different contexts? How can we use technology to transform the future of learning?

Students love that our program combines research and practice. First, you’ll dive into the science of how people learn. Then you’ll explore how to use cutting-edge technology to design awesome learning experiences. Our faculty will guide you every step of the way.

“Society is permeated with technology,” said Mimi Recker, ITLS faculty. “There’s so much interest in this field because people use it to live, collaborate, and learn.”

History

Researching Instructional Media for over 50 Years

We’ve been doing this a long time. Our program started out in 1966 as the Department of Instructional Media and Library Science. At first, we focused on preparing students for careers at school libraries and media centers.

As technology evolved, so did we. In the mid 1970s, we broadened our focus to include careers in business, industry, academia, and government. Today, we work at the cutting edge of instructional technology. Our students become leaders.

"The [ITLS] department is clearly a top tier program. It is easily in the top ten and probably in the top five programs in national reputation and recognition." (Board of Regents External Reviewers Report, 2000).

According to the 2019 U.S. News and World Report, our online graduate program is ranked 3rd in the nation.

Research Interests

Our faculty are active researchers! The ITLS department receives the most research funding in our college. We offer four years of guaranteed funding to highly qualified PhD students to join in the work. Check out the faculty pages to learn more about our faculty’s individual interests.

  • instructional design theory and application
  • innovative science teaching methods
  • distance and adult education
  • digital libraries
  • instructional games and simulations
  • virtual tutors
  • educational psychology
  • technology in STEM education
  • social-cultural learning
  • Human-computer interactions

Projects

Our faculty run a wide variety of research projects and labs here at USU. Topics include education, computer science, human-computer interaction (HCI), information sciences, engineering, media studies, cognitive science, and psychology.

CANVALYTICS

Apply computational approaches from data sciences and educational data mining research to mine and understand usage data collected by Canvas, the Learning Management System (LMS) used by Utah State University. Then apply innovative data mining algorithms to this complex dataset to better understand how instructors and students use Canvas for more or less productive outcomes.
