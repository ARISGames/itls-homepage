Doctor of Philosophy

Our PhD degree program is designed for students who wish to be better prepared for the following careers:

  • Research at colleges and universities
  • Teaching in education and corporate training
  • Research and development for public or private educational agencies or in the corporate sector

Our PhD program is one of the leading educational technology programs in the nation. It is comprised of core, methods, practicum, dissertation, and elective courses.

Students should work with a faculty advisor/chair to determine a program of study early in their experience. To increase the probability of completing the program successfully, we urge students, whenever possible, to spend a minimum of two years on campus in Logan, Utah.

Degree Requirements

  • 43 or more semester credits beyond the master's degree.
  • Completed within eight years of matriculation.
  • For PhD students holding a master’s degree in a field other than instructional technology or learning sciences: Complete or account for the ITLS master’s core classes with the exception of 6510 (up to 9 credits), resulting in a 52 credit program.
  • For PhD students who do not have a master’s degree: Complete a 70 credit PhD, including the ITLS master’s core classes.
  • Complete a minimum of 33 semester credits through USU from an approved program of study.
  • Fulfill Doctoral Residency Requirements. Graduate Degree Requirements.
  • For Utah Residency, please see: USU Residency Information.
  • Maintain a minimum GPA of 3.0, required for all USU ITLS graduate degrees.

Program Pathway

  1. Acceptance to the program.
  2. Work with your advisor or chair early in the program to determine your program of study.
  3. Select your classes: You must be admitted to the ITLS PhD program to enroll in any required PhD core courses.
